Personal History

I completed my B.S. in physics from the Honors Tutorial College at Ohio University in 2022. My undergraduate thesis involved the analysis of in-beam gamma-ray spectroscopy data from NSCL in 2019. My analysis focused on high-spin 66Fe, of interest due to its location in the N=40 Island of Inversion.

In the fall of 2022 I began my PhD in the Nuclear Engineering department at UC Berkeley. My dissertation involves coulomb excitation of neutron-rich Fe.

In my free time, I like to make art, run/canicross, and go camping with my wife and our dog.
