About Me

I'm an undergraduate at Ohio University's Honors Tutorial College studying physics. My core physics classes are intensive one-on-one tutorials with a faculty member that are tailored to cover additional materials in which I express interest. Due to these courses, I have gained valuable experience in programming, practice in writing, and opportunities to connect with other scientists who have aided me in my research.

My interest lies in nuclear physics, more specifically in low energy nuclear structure. The goal of studying low energy nuclear structure is to understand the way that neutrons and protons arrange themselves to form a nucleus, which is sometimes accompanied with surprising conclusions, such as the existence of football- or pear-shaped nuclei. The best way to develop models that are consistent with real-life behavior of nuclei is to base them on our measurements (in this situation, energies contained in gamma-decay spectra)—not only of naturally occurring isotopes, but also particularly massive or unstable nuclei. I plan to continue in the effort to experimentally determine the structure of these exotic and unstable nuclei that exist on the edge of what is physically possible. The fabrication of medical isotopes for radiation therapies or improvements to detector technology for industrial use are two applications of the knowledge that I hope to generate. My desire is to contribute to laying down the scientific foundation so that others are able to pursue and develop the technologies mentioned above.

Since the summer of 2020, I have been working with the low energy nuclear structure group at Lawrence Berkeley National Lab in Berkeley, California. My research involves gamma-ray spectroscopy—the study of the distinct energies of photons, or light particles, emitted from nuclei as they transition to a lower energy state. With the gamma-ray energies measured in our experiments, I can learn about the structure of the decaying nuclei and contribute to a more comprehensive understanding of systems of nucleons.
